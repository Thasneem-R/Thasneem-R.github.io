How microgravity affects bones

Bones can lose density in space. This tutorial explains it with simple diagrams and what astronauts do to reduce bone loss.

Summary of physiological changes, countermeasures, and links to research (NTRS, Human Research Program). Includes references to bone-density datasets and exercise protocols.

Short explanation: in microgravity, bones no longer bear weight so the body reduces bone mass — astronauts exercise daily to reduce this.

Growing plants on the ISS

See how astronauts grow lettuce and other plants in space using special plant habitats like Veggie and the Advanced Plant Habitat.

Links to GeneLab omics datasets and Veggie experiment logs for analysis. Includes recommended dataset IDs to explore.

Short story: plants have been grown in space to study how they respond to microgravity and to learn how to grow food on future missions.

Microbes in space

Small organisms like bacteria behave differently in space. Learn why scientists study them on the ISS.

Contains links to microbial experiments, sequence datasets, and microbiome studies archived at NASA portals.

Short summary: studying microbes helps keep astronauts healthy and informs planetary protection policies.
